Game Tokenomics Consulting Service​

TripleC, as an incubator and publisher of games, helps game developers design their tokenomics and provides an SDK for implementation. Most game developers, especially those making traditional web2 games, do not know about blockchain, crypto, and tokenomics. They also probably do not want to learn about the new web3 tech stack because they want to focus on making good games in terms of content, graphics, playability, etc. Yet, tokenomics is a crucial part of web3 games. It is different for each game. TripleC will advise the web2 game developers to design token economies that are customized to their games.

Tokenomics is defined as the supply and demand characteristics of a cryptocurrency project. It is central to the in-game economy of a web3 game. Tokenomics is to a game token as the central bank’s monetary policy is to the fiat currency. The tokenomic policy determines how a particular token is allocated (schedule) and what the tokens are for (utility). The schedule and the utility in turn influence the supply and demand, respectively, and hence the price of the token. There are many details that go into a particular tokenomic ecosystem. TripleC will assist game developers to navigate the myriad of decisions they need to make to create web3 games. The main decisions are as follows.

Mining vs Staking​

Mining, as in Bitcoin (BTC) and Ethereum 1.0 (Eth1), gives new tokens to those gamers who devote computer resources to validate transactions and add them as new blocks on the chain in a decentralized network of computers. This is also known as proof-of-work. Staking, as in Ethereum 2.0 (Eth2), rewards those gamers who lock up some tokens in a smart contract to validate new transactions. If they improperly validate bad or fraudulent data, they will be penalized by losing part or all of the stake. This is also known as proof-of-stake. Proof-of-Stake (PoS) is more scalable because it is much more energy efficient, whereas proof-of-Work (PoW) is more robust because the network cannot be easily “bought” by the largest stakeholders.

To Burn or Not to Burn, When to Burn​

While some cryptos like Bitcoin do not have a token-burning program, many others such as Ethereum (starting in August 2021) and Binance’s BNB do burn tokens to reduce the quantity in circulation. According to the law of supply and demand, reducing the availability of tokens shall, theoretically and all else fixed, increase the price of the token, at least in short term. For instance, new investors may enjoy burning after a successful launch of ICO because their tokens will appreciate and be less subject to over circulation. Also, a token, such as KuCoin, that has a periodic burning scheme may be more attractive to token holders because the value of their digital assets increases from time to time.

There are several ways to burn tokens. The simplest is to buy back tokens and send them to a frozen private address where there is no private key. The tokens sent can never be recovered or withdrawn. They no longer exist. Alternatively, with every transaction, the sender pays a small fee using the tokens, which are then destroyed in the process. Ethereum collects the gas fees generated from transactions and burns those Ethereum.

Limited vs. Unlimted Supplies​

An important part of a tokenomic ecosystem is to specify the supply mechanics. A limited supply token caps the number of tokens that can and will ever exist at a maximum. After that, the circulating supply can only decrease thru burning, loss, stash, etc. For example, the number of Bitcoins that will ever be minted is 21 million. In addition, the issuance rate specifies how many tokens are added to the ecosystem each period. From 2016 to 2020, there were 12.5 new Bitcoins created every 10 minutes. After May 11th, 2020, the minting dropped to 6.25 Bitcoins per the same duration. This is equivalent to that roughly every 4 years the issuance rate is halved. This process continues until all 21 million Bitcoins are minted around the year 2140, after which no more Bitcoins will be created.

A limited supply token together with a burning schedule defines a deflationary ecosystem. A deflationary system is one such that the total number of tokens decreases over time. BNB is an example. The main benefit of a deflationary ecosystem is token price appreciation through scarcity.

On the other hand, an unlimited supply token is an inflationary system in which new tokens are entering into circulation all the time. There is no limit to how many tokens will exist. Eighteen million Ethers are mined each year. Given enough time, there can be an arbitrarily large number of Ethers available. However, this does not mean that Ether will become worthless. First, the inflation rate decreases over time. Suppose there are 120 million Ethers now. The inflation rate is 18/120=15%. In 10 years, there will be 120+（18 x 10）= 300 million Ethers. The inflation rate will be down to 18300=6%\frac{18}{300}=6 \%30018​=6%. That is, for time TTT,

Token Allocations and Vesting Schedules​

In the web3 game space, game developers and founders often mint tokens and sell them to investors to raise funding to support the projects. There is often a waiting period before they start to receive the tokens and can sell them for money. Otherwise, if they all received the tokens at the same time, that would create a surge in the circulating supply and hence a fall in token price. If they all sold the tokens at the same time, that would create even a bigger and sudden price drop (and loss of confidence) in the token. A careful token allocation and release or vesting schedule are critical to minimizing the impact on circulating supply and token price, hence maintaining a healthy economy.

Vesting is the process of locking and distributing purchased tokens according to a predefined schedule. Cliff is the period when the tokens are locked before a vesting schedule starts. This scheme intends to delay access to the assets being offered. There are two main vesting schedules.

Linear Vesting​

Using a linear vesting schedule, tokens are distributed in equal parts for some time. For example, 10,000 CCCGs are distributed each quarter for 4 quarters for a total of 40,000 CCCGs. If a cliff of 6 months is added, then the first batch of tokens will be transferred in the 7th month for 4 consecutive quarters. See Figure 1

Twisted Vesting​

We can design a customized vesting schedule to suit a particular purpose. For example, 24% of the tokens are distributed monthly in the first 3 months, followed by a cliff of 6 months, then the rest of the 76% are distributed after. In addition, for the same vesting schedule, e.g., quarterly distribution, using different cliffs for different parties can spread out the releases. For example, party A receives tokens in Jan, April, July, and October; while party B receives tokens in Feb, May, August, and November.

TripleC as the Custodian​

The simplest method to distribute tokens to stakeholders according to a vesting schedule is to have the game developers hold onto all these tokens and then airdrop them to the stakeholders on the release dates specified by the schedule. However, this method is insecure. The game developers have control over a large number of tokens. They can sell them off beforehand or they may cancel the release altogether. It is entirely up to them to honor the agreement (or not).

TripleC solves this trust problem by acting as a third-party custodian between the game developers and the stakeholders. We will audit their smart contracts for the vesting schedules and certify their validity. We will hold onto those reserved tokens and release them according to the schedules. In addition, TripleC will provide an API and an interface to facilitate the creation of customized vesting schedules. See Figure 3.

By leveraging TripleC as a trusted platform to act as the custodian of tokens, the advisor, auditor, and administrator of a good vesting schedule, we help prevent market dumping and token price manipulation as well as increase transparency. This in turn will help fundraising of the projects because the investors have more faith in the security of their investment.

Game Economy​

A web3 game economy mirrors the real-world economy as it also has a real economy and a financial economy. Game tokens are the fiat currency in the virtual world, whereas non-fungible tokens (NFTs) signify ownership of in-game assets. NFTs can represent any digital assets such as virtual land, houses, avatars, pets, weapons, and costumes. Some provide certain benefits to the gamers, e.g. powerful magical weapons, making them progress faster; some are purely cosmetic, e.g., avatars and costumes, and do not affect gameplay. The in-game real economy concerns the production of game tokens and NFTs, purchase and flow of goods (weapons and armor) and services (mercenaries, PvP) within the game. Gamers produce game tokens (or earn “wages”) and NFTs by “working” such as completing quests, battling other players, and progressing through the game levels. They can also trade tokens and NFTs with other gamers, just like in the real-world real economy.

Decentralized Exchanges (DEX)​

The limit order book mechanism has been proven effective and efficient by hundreds of years of trading experience of numerous stocks and assets worldwide. However, it is not popular in the crypto world because LOB assumes liquidity of the assets traded. There are counterparties on both the bid and ask sides all the time. Many tokens are sparsely traded. When you want to buy (or sell), there may not be a counterparty to trade with you. To solve this problem, the crypto world creates these automated market-making (AMM) robots that are always ready to trade with you and quote you a price for the tokens. Moreover, anyone with tokens can create these robots. These so-called decentralized exchanges are basically liquidity pools (LP) together with AMMs.

All DEXes use a deterministic function to determine the price of a token pair. Uniswap pioneers the Constant Product Market Makers (CPMM). There are other variants that are similar. Notable ones are Constant Sum Market Makers, Constant Mean Market Makers, and Curve (a.k.a. Stableswap) AMM.

However, these kinds of pricing functions like CPMM are economically flawed. The first fundamental problem is that it is very easy to front-run an order. After an order is submitted to an LP, it is authenticated but not settled. It is important to note that the settlement price is determined by the pricing formula, hence the xxx and yyy reserves, at the time of settlement not the time of submission. Thus, during the pending of settlement, anyone who has access to the LP can submit the same order but with a higher fee for a higher priority for execution, and then another order at a later time at a lower fee to reverse and close the position. A limit order book has no such problem as all price information is transparent to all at all times.

The second fundamental problem is that these AMMs and pricing functions do not concern about the “true” prices of tokens and thus arbitrage opportunities abound at the cost of investors. CCCG is pegged to a dynamic basket of game tokens. Volatility​

Sometimes, there is a misunderstanding that a token price will not grow if it has small volatility. This is not true. μ\muμ and σ\sigmaσ are two independent parameters of a (stochastic) process. For example, a staircase function that increases by 1 each day can grow indefinitely with zero volatility (zero uncertainty), hence large μ\muμ and small σ\sigmaσ. A constant zero function has small μ\muμ and small σ\sigmaσ. A white noise process can have small μ\muμ and large σ\sigmaσ. The S&P 500 index can have large μ\muμ and large σ\sigmaσ. In general, we want CCCG to have a large growth rate μ\muμ and small volatility σ\sigmaσ.

Conclusions for CCCG Value Model​

We have developed a new token that has two much-desired properties in the crypto world:

  1. Grow with the ecosystem.

  2. Small volatility.

Triple C achieves this by making the innovation of the “dynamic basket token”. The ecosystem fiat currency CCCG is pegged to a basket of tokens of the top games published on our platform. In operation, anyone can exchange 1 CCCG with Triple C for a pre-specified number of game tokens but not the other way round. Gamers can still trade game tokens for CCCG (or any other game tokens) in the marketplace but not necessarily with Triple C. This mechanism essentially guarantees a lower bound to the CCCG value, backed by the real economy and the dollar values of the game tokens that CCCG is pegged to. The price appreciation of CCCG will depend on the growth of the Triple C ecosystem, circulation/liquidity premium, and the expected price appreciation of the underlying game tokens.

Our mathematics and simulations show that CCCG is resistant to the notorious death spiral where a game token can lose much of its value either rapidly or gradually. CCCG, being a dynamic basket of tokens, can only enter the death spiral state when most of all its component tokens enter such a state, which is a very low probability event. Our simulation of a particular basket policy confirms this with empirical evidence.

To construct such a token as CCCG, the issuer needs to

  1. Have a basket of tokens that are tied to the real economy. They need to have intrinsic value due not to speculation.

  2. Own a reserve of these tokens to be able to honor the commitment of pegging and exchange.

Triple C is in a unique position to fulfill these conditions. First, Triple C is in the digital game industry. Each digital game contributes to the GDP of the real economy. Playing and paying for a game is worth real USD. Second, Triple C is a digital game publisher. By providing programming infrastructure to developers, promoting and publishing games, Triple C naturally owns a percentage of the game tokens for our services. Triple C’s advantageous positions cannot be easily replicated or superseded.
